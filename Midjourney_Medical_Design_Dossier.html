01What it actually is

Midjourney — the AI image company — announced a new division, Midjourney Medical, on 17–18 June 2026. It is a hard pivot from software into medical hardware and physical hospitality. Two products, one story.

The Scanner — "Ultrasonic CT"

A full-body imaging machine the company calls Ultrasonic CT (or "the full-body ultrasound"). You stand on a platform in a shallow pool of golden light and descend ~5 cm/sec through a ring of ~500,000 tiny elements — each acting as both a microspeaker and microphone, like a dolphin's echolocation. The wave distortions through tissue are reconstructed into a sub-millimetre 3D map.

No radiation, no magnets — "just sound, water, and 60 seconds." Claimed to rival MRI detail at ~100× speed. Built with Butterfly Network (≈40 Ultrasound-on-Chip modules per system).

The Spa — the delivery model

The scanner ships inside a flagship "Midjourney Spa": hot tubs, saunas, cold plunges, and cosy rooms with pools of golden light. The scan is framed as a side effect of a place you already want to be. First location: 300 Grant Avenue, San Francisco, opening end of 2027, with 10 scanners.

Ambition: ~50,000 scanners worldwide by ~2031 and a billion scans/month. Funded with no investors — a "community-backed research lab."

02The narrative & voice

The brand is sold as a hopeful, human, almost spiritual story, not a spec sheet. This is the most important thing to internalise: it is science framed as wonder and care. Any design that reads cold or clinical is off-brand.

The core promise

"As powerful as an MRI, and as casual as a trip to the spa."

Everything ladders to one idea: changing our relationship to our own bodies — more data, more often, with less fear, so we live longer, better lives.

Voice characteristics

  • Plain, warm, first-person plural. "We dreamed of…", "we are all Midjourney." Manifesto cadence, short declaratives.
  • Wonder over jargon. Technical ideas land through metaphor — dolphins, a choir and an audience, grains of sand, golden light.
  • Calm, not hype-y. Big numbers are stated quietly. Confidence without shouting.
  • Invitation. Community-funded framing → the reader is a participant, not a customer. CTAs: join, volunteer, tell us what the spa should be.

Lexicon to reuse

golden lightsound & water60 seconds a side effectecholocationwhenever you want a place you love goingawareness of our bodieswe are all Midjourney

Words to avoid

"Patient," "clinic," "diagnosis/diagnostic" (regulatory landmine), "machine" used coldly, fear/disease-led framing, corporate health-tech speak.

03Market & the honest caveats

Design honestly: the engineering demo is genuinely exciting, but the clinical, regulatory and economic case is unproven and is being publicly challenged. Knowing the criticism keeps the work credible and avoids overclaiming.

What critics are saying

  • Gap between demo and vision. "20 minutes is not 60 seconds; 12 subjects is not a clinical trial; no FDA clearance is not a technicality."
  • Not first. Radiologists note ultrasound-tomography systems already exist commercially (e.g. breast imaging) — pushback on "nobody's done this."
  • Physics limits. Air (lungs), bone (skull) and bowel gas scatter/block ultrasound — whole-body parity with MRI is hard.
  • Overdiagnosis & data governance. Frequent whole-body scans risk incidentalomas/false positives; little said on data handling.
  • Unit economics ("a few dollars per scan") called speculative.

Implication for design: lean into the experience, wonder and agency (all defensible) and stay clear of hard medical/diagnostic claims in any concept work.

05Light, water & atmosphere

Golden light

The single strongest brand asset. A warm, low, enveloping light — closer to candle/sunset/amniotic glow than to clinical LED. It rims bodies and edges (backlight + rim light), bleeds into bloom, and leaves large areas in protective darkness. It should feel like being held, not examined.

Water

Always present: caustics, ripples, refraction, droplets on skin, surface tension catching light. Water is the metaphor for gentleness and the literal medium of the scan. Reflections and slow ripple are signature texture.

The ring / halo

The circular sensor ring around the descending platform is the hero motif — a glowing gold circle / pool of light. This is the closest thing to a logo-able shape: a luminous ring on black.

Darkness as material

Negative space is mostly black, and intentionally so. Calm, premium, focused. Don't fill it. Let single elements float in deep field.

06Composition & camera

  • Intimate, human-scale framing. Close-ups of skin, shoulders, the back of a neck, feet entering water, a hand. Tender, never voyeuristic — bodies are anonymous, lit as form.
  • Centred, ceremonial symmetry. The descent, the ring, the platform — figures centred in a glowing circle. Ritual, not procedure.
  • Macro ↔ cosmic scale jumps. The film cuts between extreme close detail (grains, droplets, sensors) and the whole body / data-volume — reinforcing "half a million elements" awe.
  • Shallow depth of field. Soft falloff, bloomed highlights, cinematic bokeh. Photographic, filmic — not 3D-render-clean.
  • The scan view is the counterpoint. When it cuts to reconstructions/segmentations, the look flips to cool, technical, grid-and-readout — a deliberate contrast to the warm world.

07Motion & sound

  • Slow, weighted descent. The signature motion is downward and gentle (the 5cm/sec elevator). Everything eases; nothing snaps. Long, calm dissolves.
  • Crossfade as a system. Scan tiles continuously crossfade raw reconstruction ↔ AI segmentation (~1s each way, looping). This "reveal what's inside" dissolve is a reusable motion idiom.
  • Slice sweeps. Body volumes are animated slice-by-slice through the form — a clean, hypnotic scanning loop.
  • Sound design. Story is literally built on sound waves — expect tonal, resonant, water-and-frequency audio. Quiet, warm, meditative; not techy stingers.

Easing guidance: long durations (600–1200ms+), cubic-bezier(.22,.61,.36,1)-style ease-out, generous holds. Calm is the brand.

08Typography & UI

Two registers run in parallel and shouldn't be mixed carelessly.

Editorial / brand

The site and manifesto read as clean, modern, humanist sans — large, confident headlines, generous line-height, lots of black space. Warm whites on black. Quiet and premium.

Safe pairings to mock with: Inter / Söhne / Neue Haas Grotesk / ABC Diatype for the sans; a restrained serif (e.g. Lyon, Tiempos) only if you want literary warmth. Confirm the live webfont via devtools before final.

Instrument / data

Inside the machine world: amber monospace readouts — tabular numerics, measurement values glowing on black (seen as figures like temperatures/levels in the film). This is the "scanner HUD" voice.

Mock with: a mono (JetBrains Mono / SF Mono / Söhne Mono) in amber #F29E25, tabular-nums, wide tracking. Use sparingly as texture/diegetic UI.

09Materials & space (Spa)

The spa renders extend the film's world into architecture. Expect:

  • Warm minimalism — stone, wood, plaster, water; soft curved geometry; spa-luxury, not hospital.
  • Pools of golden light as the architectural signature — the scanner room reads as a serene bath, not a scan suite.
  • Wellness adjacency — hot tubs, saunas, cold plunges, cosy lounge. Reference points: high-end bathhouse / onsen / Aesop-grade retail calm, fused with subtle sci-fi.
  • Floor plan exists (multi-level) and the SF site is real (300 Grant Ave) — useful for any spatial/wayfinding concept work.

12Frame-extraction workflow

The reusable process to turn any video into a reference library. A ready-to-run script ships beside this file: extract_frames.sh. Already run once on the 1440p announcement film → 20 distinct-shot keyframes + 46 timeline frames + contact sheets + palette (in /frames and /contact_sheets).

One-time setup (macOS)

brew install yt-dlp ffmpeg imagemagick python && pip3 install pillow

Run it

./extract_frames.sh "https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ai4YvYV_gW8"

Also accepts a local file or a direct CDN URL. Tunables: EVERY=2 (grid interval), SCENE=0.30 (lower = more shots), MAXRES=1440.

What it produces

① download → ② scene-change keyframes (distinct shots) → ③ timeline grid (every N sec) → ④ contact sheets → ⑤ palette swatch PNG + hex list. Manual core, if you ever want just that:
yt-dlp -S res:1440 -f bv*+ba/b -o in.mp4 URL
ffmpeg -i in.mp4 -vf "select='gt(scene,0.3)'" -vsync vfr -q:v 2 key_%03d.jpg
ffmpeg -i in.mp4 -vf "fps=1/2,scale=1280:-1" grid_%03d.jpg

On the CDN originals: Cloudflare blocked automated pulls of the .mp4/.webm/.webp from the sandbox (403 challenge). On your own machine, sign in / open the page first, then point the script at the direct URL or the downloaded file — your browser session clears the challenge. The YouTube 4K master is an excellent stand-in either way.
